Indian Turmeric Finger Export 2026: Salem vs Nizamabad, Grades, and What Buyers Must Know

India grows over 75% of the world's turmeric and exports nearly 85% of all turmeric traded globally. But not all Indian turmeric is the same — origin, curcumin content, and finger size define the premium. This guide tells you exactly what to specify.

Why Turmeric Origin Matters

Indian turmeric is sold under several origin names — Salem, Nizamabad, Erode, Sangli, Wayanad — and each refers to a specific growing region with distinct curcumin levels, colour, and finger size. Buyers who specify only "Indian turmeric" without an origin often receive inconsistent quality across shipments.

The crop is a kharif crop sown in June–July and harvested November–February. The major trading centres are Erode (Tamil Nadu) and Nizamabad (Telangana).

Key spec: Always specify minimum curcumin content (by HPLC method) in your PO. Food manufacturers typically need 2.5–3.0%; pharma buyers need 3.5%+.

Full Technical Specification Checklist

  • Curcumin content (HPLC): Min 2.5% (food grade) or min 3.5% (pharma grade)
  • Moisture: Max 10%
  • Total ash: Max 8%
  • Volatile oil: Min 2.5%
  • Lead (Pb): Max 3 ppm
  • Aflatoxin total: Max 10 ppb (EU); max 20 ppb (Codex)
  • Sudan dye: Absent (EU, UK, USA mandatory)
  • Container load: 14–16 MT per 20ft FCL
  • HS Code: 09103020

2026 Price Outlook

Turmeric prices in 2025–26 have corrected 30–40% from the 2023 drought-driven peak (Nizamabad hit Rs22,000/quintal at the height). Current prices represent a favourable entry window. Alleppey high-curcumin grade remains tight globally as pharmaceutical demand from USA and Europe continues growing faster than supply can expand.

Primary Export Markets

Bangladesh (largest volume), USA (highest value — pharma and supplement grade), UAE, Morocco, UK, Japan, and Germany are India's top turmeric markets. The USA buys almost exclusively on curcumin content specs; Bangladesh buys on colour and price; Gulf markets buy whole finger for cooking applications.

Sourcing Recommendation

For food-industry buyers: specify Erode origin, min 2.5% curcumin, request a batch CoA. For pharmaceutical buyers: specify Salem or Alleppey, min 3.5–4.0% curcumin by HPLC, with full heavy metals and Sudan dye testing from an accredited lab.
